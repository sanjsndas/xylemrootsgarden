🍪 Cookies & Digital Pollinators
Clear, minimal, and respectful of your garden.

At Xylem Roots Garden, cookies help us water your experience — not harvest your data. This policy explains the tiny text files we use, your control over them, and our pledge to keep tracking low-impact, just like permaculture.

Effective date: April 21, 2026  |  Last revised: April 21, 2026 🌱 GDPR & CCPA compliant cookie practices

1. What are cookies? (a gardener’s analogy)

Cookies are small text files that websites store on your device (computer, tablet, or phone) when you browse. Think of them as little botanical markers: they remember your preferences, help pages load smoothly, and tell us which articles about soil biology or native perennials are most loved — without ever reading your private thoughts. We use only essential and strictly limited analytical cookies. No creepy tracking, no behavioral ads, no selling crumbs of your data.

3. Specific cookies we set (detailed list)

To be fully transparent, here are the actual cookies you may encounter while browsing xylemroots.garden:

  • xylem_session – Essential session ID to keep you logged in (for workshop registration/forms). Expires when you close the browser.
  • xylem_cookie_preferences – Remembers your analytics consent choice. Duration: 6 months.
  • xylem_analytics_opt – Stores if you consented to anonymized usage stats. Value: 'true' or 'false'. 365 days max.
  • plausible_ignore (if applicable) – For our privacy-focused analytics; prevents counting your visit if you opt-out.
  • cf_clearance – Security cookie from Cloudflare (bot protection, strictly necessary).

We don’t use flash cookies, zombie cookies, or any other covert tracking tech. Ever.

When you first visit Xylem Roots Garden, you'll see a minimal cookie banner that explains our practices. You can:

  • ✅ Accept only strictly necessary cookies (default for full functionality).

  • ✅ Change your mind at any time via the “Cookie Preferences” link in our footer or by clicking the floating cookie icon (if active).

5. Third-party cookies – extremely limited

We embed no third-party tracking scripts. However, we sometimes include gardening videos from YouTube (via privacy-enhanced mode) or maps for workshop locations. These embedded services may set their own cookies if you interact with them. We recommend reviewing their policies, but we choose privacy-enhanced modes where possible. For YouTube, we use youtube-nocookie.com embeds to minimize tracking until you press play. No external ad networks or social plugins are present.

6. Analytics with integrity – no personal fingerprints

Our analytical cookies never store your IP address, user ID, or any identifying parameters. We use aggregated metrics such as: which articles about regenerative soil are trending, how many unique visitors per week, and general geographic region (country/state). We cannot and do not track individuals across the web. Our analytics provider (self-hosted or compliant partner) is contractually forbidden from reselling data. We consider analytics a tool for improving the garden — not a surveillance device.

7. Do Not Track (DNT) & Global Privacy Control

We honor Do Not Track signals sent by your browser. If your browser sends a DNT:1 header, we will not deploy any optional analytics cookies and will respect your preference for no tracking beyond strictly necessary cookies. Additionally, we support Global Privacy Control (GPC), which automatically signals your opt-out of data sharing — and since we don’t share anyway, we respect it fully.

8. Cookie retention & updates

Cookie lifespans are kept as short as possible. Session cookies disappear when you close the browser; preference cookies last up to 6 months; anonymous analytics cookies may persist up to 14 months before auto-deletion. We will notify you of any material changes to our Cookie Policy via the website banner and update the “last revised” date. Your continued use implies acceptance of the revised policy unless you withdraw consent via preferences.

9. Children & cookie consent

We do not knowingly collect or store cookie data from children under 13. Our analytics are anonymized and cannot identify age. If you are a parent or guardian and have concerns about your child’s data, please contact us.
